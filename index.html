Current Research
Ongoing Research
Terrain-Aware Reaction–Diffusion Models and Optimal Control for Wildlife Disease Spread
Collaborative research in mathematical biology, numerical PDEs, and optimal control
Developing reaction–diffusion and optimal control models for wildlife rabies spread on complex domains where terrain influences animal movement. Current work investigates computational methods for heterogeneous domains and control strategies for limiting disease spread.
Ongoing Research
Boundary Integral Methods for Interface Problems
Scientific computing and numerical PDEs
Investigating boundary integral formulations for elliptic interface problems, with applications to electrostatic models and PDEs on complex geometries.Current work focuses on numerical implementation, validation, and analysis of interface formulations.

Talks & Presentations
WWU Mathematics Colloquium Talk
An Introduction to Spatiotemporal Rabies Models
Gabe Flanagin & Alex Escoto · WWU Mathematics Colloquium Series · April 2026
Gave an introduction to reaction-diffusion based models used to study the spatial spread of infectious diseases in wildlife. Also introduced and numerically solved optimal control problems trying to minimize the spread of such diseases. Currently working with collaborator to submit novel results which generalize these models to complex domains with terrain that influences animal movement.

Senior Project Presentation
Using Max-Plus Algebra to Compute Reachability in Dynamic Systems
Gabe Flanagin, advised by Rick Barnard · WWU Mathematics Scholars Week · May 2025
Studied and implemented a max-plus algebra based algorithm for approximating solutions to Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman equations. Proved the method is applicable to a broader class of functions than previously shown and generalized semiconvexity results underlying the approach.
Poster Presentation
Investigating the Atomic Ordering, Chemical Bonding, and Electronic Structure of a Class of ABX₂ Semiconductors for Solar Energy Conversion
Gabe Flanagin · WWU Scholars Week Poster Session · May 2025
Presented work investigating atomic ordering, chemical bonding, and electronic structure in a class of ABX₂ semiconductors for solar energy conversion. The project used density functional theory to study how geometric structure influences electronic properties and material behavior.
